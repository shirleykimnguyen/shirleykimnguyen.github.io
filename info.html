about

SHIRLEY KIM NGUYEN

You know the word, singing? Say, Sing-wee-yen. Now, take out the si and start the word with that ng sound, Ng‑wee‑yen. That's how you pronounce my name.

Born Kim Hoàng, Shirley is an actor originally from Ho Chi Minh City, Vietnam. At five, she first became interested in acting after watching the 2001 biopic of Shirley Temple, whom she found inspiring for singing, dancing and acting simultaneously at such a young age, leading to her adopting the name Shirley for herself in first grade English class in public elementary school in Ho Chi Minh, Vietnam.

After an unexpected move to the US at 11 years old with her family, Shirley lived in Georgia for 8 years, during which she shied away from her interest in acting, having internalised the effects of lack of media representation. Between moving to California and completing her undergraduate studies at UC Irvine in English and Spanish, she began her acting training after watching Crazy Rich Asians catalysed her into motion.

Shirley is currently training with Actor’s Studio of Orange County (ASOOC) and Howard Fine. She has starred in Miguel S Romero’s short film, Ceci N’est Pas Une Pomme. She is currently one of ten actors chosen for the Actor’s Studio of Orange County’s showcase which recently launched.

When she’s not acting, Shirley spends her time surfing, playing piano, and self-learning languages.
